## Business Entity Setup

### Entity Type Selection

| Entity Type | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|------------|------|------|----------|
| **Sole Proprietorship** | Simple setup, minimal paperwork | Personal liability, harder to scale | Testing the market |
| **LLC (Limited Liability Company)** | Liability protection, tax flexibility | More paperwork, state fees | Most consultants |
| **S-Corp** | Tax savings on self-employment tax | Complex compliance, payroll required | Higher revenue ($75K+) |
| **C-Corp** | Best liability protection, investment-ready | Double taxation, most complex | Venture-backed growth |

**Recommendation**: Start with **LLC**, convert to S-Corp when revenue exceeds $75K/year.

### Business Registration Checklist

- [ ] **Choose business name**
  - Check availability in your state
  - Check domain name availability
  - Verify no trademark conflicts

- [ ] **Register business entity**
  - File with Secretary of State
  - Obtain Certificate of Formation
  - Cost: $50-500 depending on state

- [ ] **Obtain EIN (Employer Identification Number)**
  - Free from IRS (irs.gov)
  - Required for business bank account
  - Required for hiring contractors

- [ ] **Register for state/local business licenses**
  - Business license (city/county)
  - Professional license (if required in your state)
  - Home occupation permit (if working from home)

- [ ] **Register for state taxes**
  - Sales tax (if applicable to services)
  - State income tax withholding (if hiring)

## Legal and Insurance

### Insurance Requirements

**Professional Liability (Errors & Omissions) Insurance**:
- **Required**: Yes - protects against claims of negligence, mistakes, or failure to deliver
- **Recommended coverage**: $1-2 million per occurrence
- **Estimated cost**: $1,000-3,000/year
- **Providers**: Hiscox, CNA, Hartford, Chubb

**General Liability Insurance**:
- **Required**: Often required by clients
- **Coverage**: Bodily injury, property damage, personal injury
- **Recommended coverage**: $1 million per occurrence, $2 million aggregate
- **Estimated cost**: $500-1,500/year
- **Often bundled**: With E&O insurance

**Cyber Liability Insurance**:
- **Recommended**: Yes - given your security focus
- **Coverage**: Data breaches, cyber attacks, ransomware
- **Recommended coverage**: $1 million
- **Estimated cost**: $1,000-2,500/year

### Insurance Checklist

- [ ] Obtain Professional Liability (E&O) quotes from 3+ providers
- [ ] Obtain General Liability quotes
- [ ] Consider Cyber Liability insurance
- [ ] Review policy exclusions carefully
- [ ] Ensure coverage meets typical client requirements ($1-2M)
- [ ] Keep Certificate of Insurance (COI) ready for clients

### Legal Documents

**Essential Contracts**:

1. **Master Services Agreement (MSA)**
   - [ ] Defines overall relationship with client
   - [ ] Terms and conditions
   - [ ] Liability limitations
   - [ ] Intellectual property rights
   - [ ] Confidentiality provisions

2. **Statement of Work (SOW) Template**
   - [ ] See [SOW_TEMPLATE.md](../templates/SOW_TEMPLATE.md)
   - [ ] Customize for each engagement
   - [ ] Clear scope and deliverables
   - [ ] Payment terms

3. **Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)**
   - [ ] Mutual NDA template

4. **Independent Contractor Agreement**
   - [ ] For when you hire subcontractors

**Legal Review**:

## Pricing Strategy

### Rate Calculation

**Target Annual Revenue**: $200,000-400,000 (first year goal)

**Billable Hours Calculation**:
```
Total work hours/year: 2,080 (40 hrs Ã— 52 weeks)
Non-billable time:
- Marketing/Sales: 20%
- Admin/Operations: 15%
- Professional Development: 10%
- Vacation/Sick: 5%
- Total non-billable: 50%

Billable hours: 2,080 Ã— 50% = 1,040 hours/year
```

**Hourly Rate Calculation**:
```
Target Revenue: $300,000
Billable Hours: 1,040
Hourly Rate: $300,000 Ã· 1,040 = $288/hour

Recommended Range: $250-400/hour
```

### Pricing Models

**1. Time & Materials (T&M)**
- Bill hourly/daily rate
- Best for: Uncertain scope, ongoing support
- Rate: $250-400/hour or $2,000-3,200/day

**2. Fixed Price**
- Defined scope, fixed fee
- Best for: Clear requirements, repeatable work
- Include contingency: 15-20%
- Example: Assessment = $15,000 fixed

**3. Retainer**
- Monthly fee for allocated hours
- Best for: Ongoing support relationships
- Typical: $5,000-15,000/month for 20-60 hours
- Unused hours: Don't roll over (or cap rollover)

**4. Value-Based**
- Price based on client value, not hours
- Best for: High-impact strategic work
- Example: Compliance achievement = % of potential fine avoided

### Sales Process

**Stage 1: Lead Generation**
```
Activities:
- Content marketing
- LinkedIn engagement
- Network outreach
- Speaking engagements

Goal: Generate 5-10 leads/month
```

**Stage 2: Discovery Call**
```
Duration: 30-45 minutes
Objectives:
- Understand client challenges
- Assess fit
- Explain your approach
- Determine next steps

Questions to Ask:
- What challenges are you facing?
- What have you tried?
- What does success look like?
- What's your timeline?
- What's your budget range?
```

**Stage 3: Proposal**
```
Components:
- Executive summary
- Understanding of requirements
- Proposed approach
- Timeline and milestones
- Investment (pricing)
- Team/qualifications
- Terms and conditions

Turnaround: 3-5 business days
```

**Stage 4: Negotiation & Close**
```
Activities:
- Address objections
- Negotiate terms
- Finalize SOW
- Obtain signatures
- Collect deposit (25-50%)
```

### First Client Strategy

**Approach for First 3 Clients**:

1. **Leverage existing network**
   - Former employers who know your work
   - Colleagues who moved to new companies
   - Industry contacts

2. **Consider reduced rates initially**
   - 10-20% discount for first clients
   - In exchange for case study permission
   - In exchange for testimonial/reference

3. **Start with smaller engagements**
   - Assessments (lower risk for client)
   - Training (demonstrable value quickly)
   - Staff augmentation (foot in the door)

4. **Over-deliver**
   - Exceed expectations
   - Build reference-able relationship
   - Ask for referrals
